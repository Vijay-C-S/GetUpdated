What is Cognizant? A Quick Overview

Before we talk about the hiring process, let me give you a quick background. Cognizant is an American multinational IT company headquartered in New Jersey, USA. But here's the thing � a massive chunk of their workforce is based right here in India. We're talking about 300,000+ employees in India alone!

They work with some of the biggest companies in the world � banks, healthcare companies, retail giants, you name it. From software development to cloud computing, AI to digital transformation � Cognizant does it all.

Quick Facts About Cognizant

  • Founded: 1994 (as a division of Dun & Bradstreet)
  • Headquarters: Teaneck, New Jersey, USA
  • India HQ: Chennai, Tamil Nadu
  • Employees: 350,000+ globally
  • Revenue: $19.4 Billion (2025)
  • Major Offices in India: Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Kolkata, Mumbai

Eligibility Criteria � Can You Apply?

Before you get excited, let's check if you're actually eligible. Cognizant has pretty straightforward requirements, but they're strict about them.

Basic Requirements:

  • Education: B.E / B.Tech / MCA / M.Tech / M.Sc (CS/IT)
  • Percentage: Minimum 55% or 5.5 CGPA (no active backlogs)
  • Year of Passing: 2025 / 2026 batch
  • Gap: Maximum 1 year gap allowed in education
  • Age: No specific age limit mentioned

Branch-wise Eligibility:

  • GenC: All engineering branches + MCA + M.Sc (CS/IT)
  • GenC Next: CS, IT, ECE, EEE, E&I, and related branches
  • GenC Elevate: CS, IT branches with strong coding skills

Important Note

If you have active backlogs at the time of application, you won't be eligible. Clear them first! Also, if you've already appeared for Cognizant in the last 6 months, you might not be able to reapply immediately.

Cognizant Hiring Process � Step by Step

Now comes the important part. Let me walk you through each round of the selection process. I'll be honest � it's not super difficult, but you need to prepare properly.

Round 1: Online Application

Register on Cognizant careers portal or apply through your college's placement cell. Fill in your details accurately � they verify everything!

Round 2: Aptitude Test (90 minutes)

Quantitative ability, logical reasoning, verbal ability. Around 55-60 questions. Difficulty level is moderate � practice from IndiaBix, PrepInsta.

Round 3: Coding Assessment (60 minutes)

2-3 coding questions on their platform. Languages allowed: Java, Python, C, C++. Focus on arrays, strings, basic algorithms. GenC Elevate has harder questions.

Round 4: Communication Assessment

Written English test + short essay. They check grammar, vocabulary, and how well you can express yourself. Not too tough if your English is decent.

Round 5: Technical + HR Interview

Usually combined into one round (30-45 mins). Basic tech questions + HR questions. They focus on your projects, internships, and communication skills.

How to Crack Cognizant Interview � My Honest Tips

Look, I'm not going to give you some generic advice like "be confident" or "dress well." You already know that. Here's what actually matters:

For the Aptitude Round:

  • Practice at least 50 questions daily from topics like time & work, percentages, averages, and data interpretation
  • Use a timer � you get roughly 1.5 minutes per question
  • Don't get stuck on one question. Mark it and move on
  • Websites to use: PrepInsta, IndiaBix, GeeksforGeeks

For the Coding Round:

  • Master these topics: Arrays, Strings, Sorting, Searching, Basic recursion
  • Solve at least 100 easy-medium problems on LeetCode or HackerRank
  • For GenC: Focus on pattern printing, basic logic problems
  • For GenC Next/Elevate: Add DSA basics � linked lists, stacks, queues

For the Interview:

  • Know your resume inside out. If you've mentioned a project, be ready to explain every line of code
  • Prepare STAR format answers for behavioral questions (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  • Common questions: "Tell me about yourself," "Why Cognizant?", "Explain your final year project"
  • Ask questions at the end � shows genuine interest

Golden Tip

During the interview, if you don't know something, just say "I haven't worked on that yet, but I'm eager to learn." Honesty > Fake answers. Interviewers appreciate candidates who are genuine about their knowledge gaps.

1. Campus Placement (Recommended)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there any bond at Cognizant?

Yes, Cognizant typically has a 2-year service agreement. If you leave before that, you might have to pay a certain amount (usually ₹50,000 - ₹1,00,000 depending on the program).

What is the training period like?

Cognizant provides 2-3 months of training for freshers. You'll learn technologies based on your project allocation. During training, you get a stipend (approximately 60-70% of your salary).

Can non-CS students apply for GenC Next?

It depends on the drive. Some GenC Next drives are open to ECE and EEE students too. Always check the eligibility criteria for that specific drive.

How long does it take to get results?

Usually 2-4 weeks after the interview. For campus placements, results might come faster (sometimes within a week).

Is work from home available?

Cognizant follows a hybrid model in 2026. You might need to come to office 2-3 days a week, depending on your project and client requirements.

Final Thoughts

Look, getting into Cognizant isn't rocket science. Thousands of freshers crack it every year. The key is consistent preparation and not taking it lightly just because it's an "easy" company to get into.

Start preparing at least 1-2 months before your campus drive. Solve problems daily, work on your communication skills, and most importantly � be genuine in your interview.

Cognizant is a great place to start your career. Good work-life balance, decent pay, lots of learning opportunities, and a huge employee base means you'll never feel alone.
