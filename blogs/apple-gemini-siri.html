Apple to Unveil Gemini-Powered Siri in February

January 25, 2026 5 min read GetUpdated Team

In what could be the most significant upgrade to Siri since its launch in 2011, Apple is reportedly preparing to unveil a completely reimagined voice assistant powered by Google's Gemini AI technology. According to multiple sources familiar with the matter, the announcement is expected at a special event in February 2026.

Why This Partnership is Groundbreaking

For years, Apple has been criticized for Siri lagging behind competitors like Google Assistant and Amazon's Alexa in terms of conversational abilities and contextual understanding. While Apple has made incremental improvements, the gap seemed to be widening rather than closing.

The decision to partner with Google for Siri's AI backbone represents a significant strategic shift for Apple. Historically, the company has preferred to develop technologies in-house, but the rapid advancement of large language models has apparently convinced leadership that collaboration is the faster path forward.

What We Know So Far

Based on reports from industry insiders, here's what we can expect from the new Siri:

  • Natural Conversations: The new Siri will be able to maintain context across multiple exchanges, remembering what you discussed earlier in the conversation
  • Complex Task Handling: Instead of simple commands, Siri will understand and execute multi-step requests like "Book a restaurant for tomorrow, but check my calendar first to find a free slot"
  • Deeper App Integration: Third-party apps will have more ways to hook into Siri's capabilities
  • Improved Accuracy: Fewer misunderstandings and more relevant responses
  • On-Device Processing: Privacy-focused features will still run locally on your device

The Privacy Question

Apple has long positioned itself as the privacy-focused alternative to Google and other tech giants. This partnership naturally raises questions about how user data will be handled when queries are processed using Google's technology.

Sources indicate that Apple has negotiated strict data handling agreements with Google. The Gemini model used by Apple will reportedly be a custom version that doesn't share user data with Google's other services. Additionally, Apple will continue to process sensitive requests on-device whenever possible.

"Apple remains committed to user privacy. The partnership with Google is specifically designed to improve Siri's capabilities while maintaining our privacy standards." - Industry Source

Impact on the Smart Assistant Market

If Apple delivers on these promises, it could significantly shake up the voice assistant landscape. Currently, many users rely on Google Assistant for complex queries and Siri for basic tasks. A more capable Siri could change that behavior.

For developers, this means new opportunities to create more sophisticated voice-enabled experiences. The improved natural language understanding should make it easier to build apps that work seamlessly with Siri.

When Can You Try It?

The February announcement is expected to detail the new Siri's capabilities and provide a timeline for rollout. Industry watchers expect the new features to arrive as part of a software update, likely iOS 19.4 or a similar point release.

Initial availability may be limited to newer devices with sufficient processing power, though Apple typically tries to support several generations of hardware.

The Bigger Picture

This move signals that even Apple, with its vast resources and talented engineering teams, recognizes that the AI landscape is moving too fast for any single company to go it alone. We're entering an era of strategic partnerships and collaborations that would have seemed unthinkable just a few years ago.

For consumers, this is ultimately good news. Competition and collaboration are driving rapid improvements in the technology we use every day. A smarter Siri benefits everyone – even those who don't use Apple devices, as it pushes competitors to improve as well.
