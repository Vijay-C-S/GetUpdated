Union Budget 2026 India - Complete Analysis & Tax Changes

January 31, 2026 by GetUpdated Team

Everything you need to know about Budget 2026 - new income tax slabs, relief for middle class, and how it affects YOUR pocket. Explained in simple language!

Wait, Is This Budget Actually Good?

Let us be real - every year we hear "historic budget" and "game-changer" but end up disappointed. So I am going to break down Union Budget 2026 in plain English (no jargon, I promise!) and tell you exactly what is changing and whether it actually helps YOU.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman presented her 8th consecutive budget on February 1, 2026, and honestly? There are some surprises this time. Let us dive in!

TL;DR (Too Long; Did Not Read)
- No tax up to Rs 4 lakh income (previously Rs 3 lakh)
- Standard deduction increased to Rs 75,000
- Section 80C limit raised to Rs 2 lakh
- New tax regime becomes more attractive
- Middle class gets actual relief (finally!)

Budget 2026 Key Highlights

Before we get into details, here are the headlines that matter:

  • Income Tax Relief: No tax on income up to Rs 4 lakh under new regime. That is Rs 1 lakh more than before!
  • Standard Deduction: Increased from Rs 50,000 to Rs 75,000 for salaried employees. Direct savings!
  • Section 80C: Limit raised from Rs 1.5 lakh to Rs 2 lakh. More room for tax-saving investments!
  • NPS Contribution: Employer contribution limit for NPS increased to 14% (from 10%) for private sector.
  • Healthcare: Ayushman Bharat coverage expanded to include senior citizens above 70 years.
  • Education: Rs 1.25 lakh crore allocated for education sector - highest ever!

Old Tax Regime (Optional)

The old regime remains unchanged. If you have significant deductions (home loan, 80C investments, HRA), you might still benefit from the old regime. Do the math or use our tax calculator!

Finally! Relief for Middle Class

I know, I know - we have heard this before. But this time there are some genuine changes that will put money back in your pocket:

Standard Deduction Increased

Earlier: Rs 50,000 - Now (Budget 2026): Rs 75,000

This Rs 25,000 increase means your taxable income directly reduces by Rs 25,000. If you are in the 30% tax bracket, that is a straight saving of Rs 7,500!

Home Buyers Rejoice!

  • Affordable Housing: Credit-linked subsidy extended till 2027
  • Stamp Duty: States encouraged to reduce stamp duty on first home purchase
  • Home Loan Interest: Section 24(b) limit continues at Rs 2 lakh for self-occupied property

Section 80C & Deduction Changes

80C Limit (Old): Rs 1.5L - 80C Limit (New): Rs 2L

The Section 80C limit has been increased to Rs 2 lakh! This is huge because 80C covers so many investments:

  • EPF (Employee Provident Fund) - Your employer deducts this
  • ELSS Mutual Funds - Tax saving with market returns
  • PPF (Public Provident Fund) - Safe, guaranteed returns
  • Life Insurance Premium - LIC and other policies
  • Children's Tuition Fees - School/college fees
  • Home Loan Principal - EMI principal component
Important Note: Section 80C deductions are only available in the Old Tax Regime. If you choose the New Tax Regime, you cannot claim these deductions.

Impact on Salaried Employees

If you are a salaried employee (like most of us!), here is exactly how Budget 2026 affects your take-home salary:

Good News

  • Higher Standard Deduction: Rs 25,000 more deduction means lower taxable income
  • NPS Benefits: Employer can now contribute up to 14% (great for retirement planning!)
  • No Change in HRA: House Rent Allowance exemption continues in old regime

Example Calculation

Let us say you earn Rs 10 lakh per annum:

  • Gross Income: Rs 10,00,000
  • Standard Deduction: - Rs 75,000
  • Taxable Income: Rs 9,25,000
  • Tax Payable (New Regime): Up to Rs 4L: Rs 0 | Rs 4L - Rs 8L: Rs 20,000 (5%) | Rs 8L - Rs 9.25L: Rs 12,500 (10%)
  • Total Tax: Rs 32,500 + 4% Cess = Rs 33,800

For Businesses & Startups

Running a business? Here is what is in store for you:

  • Startup Tax Holiday: Extended till March 2027 for eligible startups
  • Manufacturing Boost: New manufacturing companies can pay 15% corporate tax (conditions apply)
  • GST Simplification: New portal with simplified return filing (finally!)
  • MSME Support: Credit guarantee scheme extended with higher limits
Pro Tip for Freelancers: If you are earning from freelancing/consulting, the presumptive taxation limit under Section 44ADA continues at Rs 50 lakh. You can declare 50% as profit and pay tax only on that!

Frequently Asked Questions

Which tax regime should I choose - Old or New?

It depends on your deductions! If you have significant deductions (home loan interest, 80C investments, HRA), the old regime might be better. If your deductions are less than Rs 3-4 lakh, the new regime is likely more beneficial. Use a tax calculator to compare both.

When will these new tax slabs be applicable?

The new tax slabs will be effective from April 1, 2026 (Financial Year 2026-27). You will see the changes in your salary from April 2026.

Is there any change in capital gains tax?

No major changes in capital gains tax. LTCG on equity remains at 10% above Rs 1 lakh. STCG continues at 15%. Property LTCG remains at 20% with indexation benefit.

What if my income is exactly Rs 12 lakh?

With the new regime and standard deduction, your taxable income will be Rs 11.25 lakh. Your tax would be approximately Rs 52,500 + cess. That is an effective tax rate of just 4.5%!

Any changes in petrol/diesel prices?

No direct changes in fuel prices through this budget. However, the government continues to push for electric vehicles with enhanced subsidies under FAME III scheme.

The Bottom Line

Budget 2026 brings genuine relief to the middle class with higher standard deduction, increased Section 80C limits, and more attractive tax slabs. Whether you choose the old or new regime depends on your specific situation - run the numbers before deciding!
