How to Check VTU 7th Semester Results 2026

Follow these simple steps to check your results. The process is straightforward, but the VTU website can be slow during peak hours, so be patient!

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Visit the Official Portal: Go to results.vtu.ac.in
  2. Enter Your USN: Type your University Seat Number (USN) in the format 1XX21CSXXX
  3. Solve the Captcha: Enter the captcha code shown on the screen
  4. Click Submit: Hit the submit button to view your results
  5. Download/Print: Save your result for future reference

Pro Tip

If the website is slow or not loading, try accessing it during early morning hours (6-8 AM) or late night (after 11 PM). You can also try using a different browser or clearing your cache.

VTU Grading System Explained

For those who are confused about how VTU calculates grades, here's a quick breakdown of the grading system:

Grade Marks Range Grade Points Description
S 90 - 100 10 Outstanding
A 80 - 89 9 Excellent
B 70 - 79 8 Very Good
C 60 - 69 7 Good
D 55 - 59 6 Above Average
E 50 - 54 5 Average
P 40 - 49 4 Pass
F Below 40 0 Fail

SGPA Calculation Formula:

SGPA = Sum(Credit x Grade Point) / Sum(Credits)

Example: If you have 5 subjects with credits 4, 4, 3, 3, 2 and grades A, B, A, C, S respectively, your SGPA would be calculated as: (4x9 + 4x8 + 3x9 + 3x7 + 2x10) / (4+4+3+3+2) = 136/16 = 8.5

What If You Have Backlogs?

Don't panic if you have backlogs. VTU provides multiple opportunities to clear them. Here's what you need to know:

  • Supplementary Exams: VTU conducts supplementary exams where you can clear your backlogs
  • No Limit: There's no limit on the number of attempts to clear backlogs
  • Apply Early: Keep an eye on VTU website for supplementary exam notifications
  • Focus on Preparation: Identify weak areas and prepare accordingly

Important for Final Year Students

If you're in your final year with backlogs, make sure to clear them before your degree completion. Companies may ask for a backlog-free record during placements. Plan your preparation well in advance!

Tips for 8th Semester Preparation

With 7th sem done, you're now heading into your final semester! Here are some tips to make the most of it:

1. Focus on Your Project

Your final year project carries significant weightage. Start working on it seriously if you haven't already. Choose a project that showcases your skills and can be discussed in interviews.

2. Prepare for Placements

If you haven't been placed yet, now is the time to intensify your preparation. Focus on:

  • Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Core subjects of your branch
  • Aptitude and reasoning
  • Communication skills

3. Consider Higher Studies

If you're planning for higher studies (GATE, GRE, CAT), start your preparation alongside regular studies. Many students successfully manage both.

4. Build Your Online Presence

Create a strong LinkedIn profile, contribute to GitHub, and build a portfolio website. These things matter more than you think during job hunting!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: When will revaluation results be declared?

Revaluation results are typically declared 4-6 weeks after the revaluation application deadline. Keep checking the VTU results portal.

Q: How can I apply for revaluation?

You can apply for revaluation through your college within the specified deadline. The fee is usually around Rs.300-400 per subject.

Q: Is there a mobile app to check VTU results?

While there's no official VTU app, you can access the results portal through your mobile browser. The website is mobile-responsive.

Q: How do I get my consolidated marks card?

Consolidated marks cards are issued after completing all 8 semesters. You can collect it from your college or apply through VTU directly.
