Latest Notification Details

The KSP Constable Recruitment 2026 has been issued through two separate notifications, both targeting the same post — Police Constable (Civil) — but for different cadres and vacancy pools.

Notification 1 covers 596 vacancies specifically reserved for the SC cadre under the Kalyana-Karnataka region. This notification is aimed at candidates from the historically underdeveloped districts falling under the Kalyana-Karnataka Development Board (KKDB) jurisdiction. Candidates from this region with SC category certificates should look at this notification separately.

Notification 2 is the larger of the two, covering 3,395 vacancies from the general mixed cadre across Karnataka. This applies to candidates across all districts and categories, with the usual reservation structure.

Both notifications are managed by the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) and the application portal is the same for both. Candidates eligible for Notification 1 should check whether they need to apply separately or whether a single application covers both, as per the official notification documents.

Post-wise Vacancy Breakdown

All 3,991 vacancies are for the post of Police Constable (Civil). This is an entry-level post within the Karnataka State Police force and falls under the civil police wing — not the armed or special battalion units. The role involves general law enforcement, community policing, traffic management, and support duties at police stations across Karnataka.

Both male and female candidates are eligible to apply. The vacancy split between male and female candidates will be clearly defined in the official notification for each cadre. Female candidates are typically allocated a fixed percentage of seats under the women reservation policy applicable in Karnataka Police recruitments.

Candidates from specific communities including SC, ST, Category 1, Category 2A, 2B, 3A, and 3B will have reserved seats within the total vacancy pool. Ex-servicemen, sports persons, and candidates from forest areas may also have additional reservations as per the notification details.

Minimum Qualification PUC (Class 12 / 10+2) or equivalent
Recognised Boards Karnataka State Board, CBSE, ICSE, or any equivalent State Board
Higher Education Not mandatory; graduates can also apply

The good thing about this recruitment is that you only need to have cleared Class 12. A degree is not required. So if you passed your PUC from any stream — Science, Commerce, or Arts — you are eligible as long as you meet the age and other criteria. Candidates who are graduates or postgraduates can also apply; having a higher qualification does not disqualify you, it just is not a requirement.

What matters more here is your physical fitness and performance in the written test. The educational bar is deliberately set at PUC to allow a wider pool of young candidates to enter the force. Make sure your Class 12 certificates are genuine, from a recognised board, and that all your documents are consistent in name spelling and date of birth.

Selection Process

Getting selected as a Karnataka Police Constable is not just about cracking a written exam. The selection process is physical and medical as well, and all four stages must be cleared. Here is how the process flows:

Stage Test Details
Stage 1 Written Test 100 objective-type questions, 90 minutes, covering General Knowledge and Current Affairs
Stage 2 Physical Standards Test (PST) Height, weight, and chest measurements as per category norms
Stage 3 Physical Endurance Test (PET) Running, long jump, and shot put events for male and female candidates separately
Stage 4 Medical Examination Vision, hearing, musculoskeletal fitness, and general health assessment

Clearing the written test does not guarantee selection. You must also pass the physical stages. Many candidates who score well on paper are rejected at the PST or PET because they did not start physical training in time. Treat all four stages as equally important from day one of your preparation.

Physical Standards Test (PST)

The Physical Standards Test checks whether candidates meet the minimum height and chest measurements required to serve in the police force. These are qualifying criteria — you either meet them or you do not. There is no scoring here.

Category Height (Male) Chest (Unexpanded / Expanded) Height (Female)
General / OBC 168 cm 86 cm / 91 cm 157 cm
SC / ST 165 cm 84 cm / 89 cm 155 cm
Candidates from Hilly / Forest Areas 165 cm 84 cm / 89 cm 155 cm
Ex-Servicemen As per service record As per service record N/A

Height and chest measurements are taken by official medical staff at designated centres. Candidates who are borderline on height should get measured at a government hospital beforehand to confirm their standing. Footwear is not allowed during measurement, and clothing must be standard. Always verify the exact standards from the official KSP notification since these can differ slightly between notification batches.

Physical Endurance Test (PET)

The PET is where physical preparation matters most. Candidates who have cleared the PST proceed to the endurance test, which evaluates running speed, jumping ability, and throwing power.

Event Male Requirement Female Requirement
Running 1600 metres within the qualifying time 800 metres within the qualifying time
Long Jump 3.80 metres (2 chances) 2.75 metres (2 chances)
Shot Put 7.26 kg shot — 5.60 metres (2 chances) 4 kg shot — 3.75 metres (2 chances)

The running event is typically the elimination round where most candidates fall out. Starting your running training at least 3 to 4 months before the PET gives you a real advantage. Build stamina gradually — start with shorter distances and increase over weeks. Do not attempt to cram running fitness into the last few weeks; it does not work that way with endurance sports.

For the shot put and long jump events, technique matters as much as raw strength. A few sessions with a trained athletics coach can significantly improve your distance and reduce the chance of fouls that disqualify your attempts.

The paper is bilingual (Kannada and English), so you can read questions in whichever language you are more comfortable with.

How to Apply Online

The entire application process is online through the Karnataka Examinations Authority (KEA) portal. Here is the step-by-step flow:

  1. Open the official KEA website at cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea/ on a desktop or laptop browser (avoid applying on a mobile phone to reduce errors).
  2. Look for the KSP Constable Recruitment 2026 notification link on the homepage or under the recruitment section.
  3. Click on New Registration if you are a first-time user. Enter your name, date of birth, email address, and mobile number exactly as they appear on your official documents.
  4. A registration number and password will be sent to your registered email and mobile. Save these immediately.
  5. Log in with your registration credentials and open the KSP Constable 2026 application form.
  6. Fill in all personal, educational, and category details carefully. Cross-check every field against your original documents before moving ahead.
  7. Upload your scanned photograph and signature in the format and file size specified on the portal. Typical requirements are JPG format with a file size between 10 KB and 50 KB — check the portal for the exact specs.
  8. Select your preferred exam centre and provide any other mandatory details as required by the form.
  9. Preview the full application. Read through every field before submitting — pay special attention to name spelling, date of birth, category, and qualification details.
  10. Submit the form and proceed to the fee payment section. Pay using net banking, debit card, credit card, or UPI.
  11. After successful payment, download and save your application confirmation slip. Take a screenshot as well. This is your proof of application.

Apply as early as possible — preferably in the first week of the registration window. Government portals experience very high traffic in the last 2 to 3 days before the deadline, and slow servers or technical glitches during peak times are not uncommon. Missing the deadline because the server was slow will not get you an extension.

Why Join Karnataka Police?

Many candidates chase private-sector jobs for the pay, but Karnataka Police offers something that most private jobs cannot — long-term stability, a clear growth path, and a role that genuinely matters to your community. Here is why this recruitment is worth serious consideration:

  • Permanent State Government Job: Once you complete training and probation, this is a permanent position — not a contract or short-term role. Karnataka Police employment comes with the full security of state government service.
  • Competitive Salary with Allowances: The base pay of ₹37,500 per month, combined with DA, HRA, and other benefits, translates to a solid and growing monthly income that keeps up with cost of living.
  • Career Growth Opportunities: Constables can progress to Head Constable, ASI (Assistant Sub-Inspector), and beyond through departmental exams, seniority-based promotions, and merit-based advancement. Some constables reach the rank of Sub-Inspector over a 10 to 15-year career.
  • Medical Benefits for Family: Government medical facilities are available for you and your family, reducing a major financial burden that affects most private-sector employees.
  • Pension Through NPS: While the Old Pension Scheme no longer applies to new government recruits, the National Pension Scheme provides a structured retirement savings mechanism that private employment rarely offers with the same structure.
  • Social Respect and Community Impact: Being part of Karnataka State Police carries real social weight. You contribute to law enforcement, public safety, and community trust — a dimension of work that most desk jobs simply cannot offer.
  • Training and Skill Development: The 9-month residential training programme builds physical fitness, legal knowledge, investigation skills, and discipline — all of which stay with you throughout your career.

Preparation Tips

Many applicants ask how to best prepare for this recruitment. Here is a practical breakdown that actually works:

For the Written Test

  • Start with Karnataka GK — this section differentiates candidates who score in the same range on national GK. Know Karnataka's districts, major rivers, historical monuments, government schemes, and famous personalities.
  • Read a daily newspaper or use a current affairs app to stay updated on events from the past six months. Focus on national news, Karnataka state news, and major international events.
  • Practice previous years' KSP Constable written test papers if available. The question pattern repeats significantly, and solving old papers tells you where to focus your energy.
  • For the reasoning and mathematics sections, practice speed. The time limit is 90 minutes for 100 questions — that is less than a minute per question. Build question-solving pace through timed mock tests.
  • Do not try to cover everything at the last minute. Study consistently for 2 to 3 hours daily over 6 to 8 weeks rather than studying frantically for 10 hours a day in the final week.

For the Physical Tests

  • Start running immediately — do not wait until after the written test result to begin physical training. Build your endurance for the 1600m (male) or 800m (female) run over at least 3 months.
  • Practice long jump on a soft surface and work on your run-up and take-off technique. Two chances are given, so learn how to maximise your second attempt if the first falls short.
  • For shot put, work with the correct weight (7.26 kg for men, 4 kg for women) under guidance. Technique — particularly grip, stance, and rotation — matters more than brute strength for clearing the qualifying distance.
  • If you are borderline on height, get your official measurement done at a government medical institution beforehand to avoid surprises at the PST venue.
  • Do not neglect flexibility and injury prevention. Pulled muscles and joint injuries in the weeks before PET can cost you months of preparation. Warm up properly before every training session.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Every recruitment cycle, a portion of eligible candidates make avoidable errors. Here are the most frequent ones and how to sidestep them:

  • Applying under the wrong notification: There are two notifications — one for the SC/Kalyana-Karnataka cadre and one for the general cadre. Read both carefully before deciding which to apply under (or whether you need to apply for both separately).
  • Mismatched document details: Name spelling, date of birth, or address inconsistencies between your Aadhaar, SSLC certificate, caste certificate, and application form can lead to cancellation during document verification.
  • Uploading the wrong photo or signature: Use a recent photograph with a plain white or light background. Do not use a photo from a phone camera in poor lighting. The signature should be done in black ink on white paper.
  • Ignoring the physical preparation: Candidates who focus only on the written test and assume they will figure out the PET later lose a lot of ground. Physical fitness takes weeks and months to build — start now.
  • Waiting until the last day to apply: Portal congestion, internet issues, or bank payment delays in the final 48 hours have cost many candidates their opportunity. Apply at least a week before the closing date.
  • Not downloading the confirmation slip: The confirmation page is your only proof that your application went through successfully. Always download it immediately after payment confirmation and keep it saved in multiple places.
  • Relying on unofficial information: Coaching centres, Telegram groups, and social media pages often circulate incorrect or outdated recruitment details. Always verify from the official KSP or KEA websites.

FAQs — Karnataka Police Recruitment 2026

1. How many total vacancies are there in Karnataka Police Recruitment 2026?

A total of 3,991 Police Constable (Civil) vacancies have been announced. These are split across two notifications — 596 under the SC/Kalyana-Karnataka cadre and 3,395 under the general mixed cadre across Karnataka.

2. What is the last date to apply for KSP Constable Recruitment 2026?

Online registration closes on July 3, 2026. The last date for fee payment is July 4, 2026. Do not wait until the deadline — apply early to avoid technical issues on the portal.

3. What educational qualification is required?

You need to have passed PUC (Class 12) or an equivalent examination from any recognised board — CBSE, ICSE, or a State Board. A degree is not required, but graduate or postgraduate candidates are also eligible to apply.

4. What is the age limit for Karnataka Police Constable 2026?

General category candidates must be between 18 and 33 years. SC, ST, and other OBC categories can apply up to 35 years. Forest area candidates and Ex-Servicemen can apply up to 38 years, while reserved-category Ex-Servicemen are eligible up to 40 years.

5. What is the application fee?

General and OBC candidates pay ₹750. SC, ST, and other reserved category candidates pay ₹500. Fees are non-refundable and must be paid online by July 4, 2026.

6. What are the stages of the Karnataka Police selection process?

There are four stages: Written Test → Physical Standards Test (PST) → Physical Endurance Test (PET) → Medical Examination. All four must be cleared in sequence. Failing any stage ends your candidacy for that cycle.

7. When is the KSP Constable Written Test 2026?

The written test is tentatively scheduled for August 2, 2026. The hall ticket will be released on the KEA portal before the exam date. Always check the official website for any changes to the schedule.

8. What is the height requirement for Karnataka Police Constable?

Male General/OBC candidates need a minimum height of 168 cm with a chest of 86/91 cm. Female General/OBC candidates need 157 cm. SC/ST candidates and those from hilly or forest areas have a relaxed height requirement of 165 cm (male) and 155 cm (female).

9. What events are in the Physical Endurance Test?

The PET includes running (1600m for men, 800m for women), long jump (3.80m for men, 2.75m for women), and shot put (7.26 kg / 5.60m for men; 4 kg / 3.75m for women). Each candidate gets two chances in jumping and shot put events.

10. What salary does a Karnataka Police Constable receive?

The pay scale is ₹37,500 to ₹76,100 per month. This is supplemented by Dearness Allowance, HRA, medical benefits, and NPS coverage. Training period comes with a stipend, and the full pay begins after joining on probation.

11. Where do I apply for Karnataka Police Constable Recruitment 2026?

Applications must be submitted at cetonline.karnataka.gov.in/kea/ — the official Karnataka Examinations Authority portal. Applications through any other website or offline mode are not valid.

12. Can female candidates apply for KSP Constable 2026?

Yes. Female candidates are eligible to apply for Karnataka Police Constable (Civil) posts. The physical standards and PET requirements are different for female candidates, with relaxed distances and lower height criteria compared to male candidates.
