Vacancy and Post Details

The SSC Stenographer 2026 notification mentions a total of 731 vacancies for Grade C and Grade D posts. The full category-wise breakup is updated by SSC separately, so you should check the official notice and the website for the final distribution once it is published.

What matters for applicants is the basic post structure. Grade C and Grade D are both stenographer roles, but the pay level, work profile, and some service conditions differ. In simple terms, Grade C generally offers a higher pay structure and broader central government exposure, while Grade D is also a strong entry point for those who want a stable government career and are ready to work on transcription-based responsibilities.

Eligibility: Education, Age, and Nationality

The eligibility section is where many candidates lose time. Read it carefully before you submit the form.

Educational Qualification

As per the notification summary, the candidate should be at least a 12th pass or have an equivalent qualification from a recognized board. If you are waiting for documents or results, do not assume eligibility. Only apply if your qualification is in place and you can produce proof during verification.

Age Limit

The age is considered as on 1st August 2026.

  • SSC Stenographer Grade C: 18 to 30 years
  • SSC Stenographer Grade D: 18 to 27 years

Relaxation applies for eligible categories such as SC, ST, OBC, PwD, ex-servicemen, and specific government employee categories as mentioned in the notification. If your category claims relaxation, use the official SSC notice to confirm the exact number of years before filling the application.

Nationality

A candidate must be one of the following: an Indian citizen, a subject of Nepal, a subject of Bhutan, or a person of Indian origin who has migrated from specified countries and intends to settle in India permanently. In certain cases, a certificate of eligibility from the Government of India is required.

In practice, this means your nationality document details should be clean and consistent. Mismatched names, unclear category certificates, or old records can create unnecessary verification issues later.

How to Apply Online Step by Step

The online form is straightforward if you prepare your documents before starting. Use this simple sequence instead of filling the application in a rush:

  1. Visit the official SSC website at www.ssc.gov.in.
  2. Open the SSC Stenographer 2026 registration link.
  3. Create a new user profile if you are applying for the first time.
  4. Enter your personal details carefully, including name, date of birth, mobile number, and email ID.
  5. Save the generated registration ID.
  6. Log in again with your registration ID, password, and date of birth.
  7. Upload the photograph and signature in the required format.
  8. Fill the remaining application details and preview the entire form.
  9. Pay the fee if applicable.
  10. Submit the form and download the confirmation page for future use.

The best habit here is simple: read the instructions twice before uploading anything. A form that is technically complete can still be rejected or delayed if the image size, document quality, or category data is wrong.

Application Fee and Correction Window

The application fee for SSC Stenographer 2026 is Rs. 100 for General and OBC candidates. SC, ST, PwD, ex-servicemen, and female candidates are exempted from payment. That makes the exam accessible, but fee exemption does not mean the application can be careless. Every detail still has to match the notification.

The correction window is available on 20th and 21st May 2026. Use it only if you truly need to fix an error. Do not treat correction as a backup plan for a rushed form. It is there to correct mistakes, not to replace basic attention.

Selection Process and Exam Pattern

The selection process has two stages:

  • Computer-Based Examination
  • Skill Test in Stenography

The CBT is the first filter, and the skill test is the qualifying stage that checks whether you can actually do the job. That second part matters a lot because stenographer roles are not about theory only. Speed, accuracy, and transcription discipline are part of the job profile.

CBT Exam Pattern

General Awareness 50 questions, 50 marks, 30 minutes
General Intelligence and Reasoning 50 questions, 50 marks, 30 minutes
English Language and Comprehension 100 questions, 100 marks, 60 minutes
Total 200 questions, 200 marks, 2 hours

There is negative marking of 0.25 marks for every wrong answer. That means guessing blindly is not a smart move. If you are unsure about a question, eliminate options first and then attempt it only if you have some logical confidence.

The minimum qualifying marks in the CBT are generally listed as 30 percent for UR, 25 percent for OBC/EWS, and 20 percent for all other categories. These are qualifying benchmarks, not a promise of selection, so your real goal should be to score comfortably above the minimum.

Skill Test in Stenography

The skill test is where many candidates succeed or fail based on practice rather than intelligence. For Grade C, the dictation speed is 100 words per minute. For Grade D, it is 80 words per minute. The dictation is for 10 minutes and is transcribed on a computer.

This stage is qualifying in nature, but that does not make it easy. You need regular shorthand practice, clean typing habits, and enough familiarity with transcription rules to avoid losing marks on silly errors. If you are preparing for this exam, start stenography practice early instead of waiting until after the CBT.

Syllabus and Preparation Strategy

The syllabus is built around three main areas: General Awareness, General Intelligence and Reasoning, and English Language and Comprehension. Since the English section carries the highest number of questions, it deserves steady daily work rather than last-minute revision.

Here is the most practical preparation approach for a beginner:

  • English: Work on grammar, vocabulary, sentence correction, comprehension, and basic reading speed.
  • Reasoning: Practice analogy, series, coding-decoding, blood relation, syllogism, and simple logic sets.
  • General Awareness: Revise current affairs, Indian polity basics, history, geography, economy, awards, and static GK.
  • Stenography: Practice shorthand daily because the skill test is speed-driven, not memory-driven.

If your preparation time is short, do not try to study everything in equal depth. Focus on the parts that offer the highest return: English accuracy, reasoning speed, and regular stenography practice. A candidate who studies a little every day usually beats a candidate who studies a lot for one weekend and then disappears for two weeks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most avoidable problems in SSC applications happen because candidates rush. Keep an eye on these mistakes:

  • Applying without checking age eligibility and category relaxation properly.
  • Using an unclear photo or signature that fails the upload requirement.
  • Entering a name or date of birth that does not match official documents.
  • Waiting until the final date to pay the fee or submit the form.
  • Ignoring shorthand practice and relying only on written-test preparation.
  • Overestimating the CBT and underestimating negative marking.

These sound small, but they are exactly the kind of mistakes that break momentum. If you avoid them early, your application process becomes much smoother.
